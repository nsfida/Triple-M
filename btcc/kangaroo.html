How it Works

  1. What G means: G is the known Generator point of the secp256k1 elliptic curve. It is the universal starting point for Bitcoin keys.
  2. What public key means: The public key P is mathematically derived by multiplying the generator by the private key: P = x * G. Finding x from P is the Discrete Logarithm Problem.
  3. What tame kangaroo does: It starts at the upper boundary b of the known range and executes a deterministic, pseudo-random walk based on point coordinates. Periodically, it drops "Distinguished Points" (markers) into a Map to record its path.
  4. What wild kangaroo does: It starts at the unknown target public point P. It uses the exact same deterministic walk logic. If its path intersects the tame kangaroo's trail, it will unwittingly follow it and hit a stored marker.
  5. What collision means: A collision occurs when Wild hits a Tame marker (or vice versa). Because their absolute curve positions are perfectly equal at that moment, we know exactly how far each kangaroo traveled to get there. We can then solve for the original starting distance x using simple arithmetic.
